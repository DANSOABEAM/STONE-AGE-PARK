About The Park

Discover the rich history, heritage, and cultural significance of Abetifi Stone Age Park — one of Ghana’s most remarkable archaeological and eco-tourism destinations.

History of The Park

Abetifi Stone Age Park is believed to preserve evidence of ancient human existence dating back thousands of years. The caves and rock formations found within the park provide insight into how early humans lived, survived, and interacted with nature during the Stone Age era. Mission & Vision

Preserving history while promoting tourism

Our Mission

To preserve and promote the archaeological, cultural, and natural heritage of Abetifi Stone Age Park while creating unforgettable educational and tourism experiences for visitors.

Our Vision

To become one of Africa’s leading historical and eco-tourism destinations, attracting visitors globally and inspiring appreciation for Ghana’s ancient heritage.

Cultural Importance

The park represents an important part of Ghana’s cultural identity and historical legacy. It highlights the relationship between ancient communities and nature while preserving stories, traditions, and heritage for future generations.

Visitors not only experience nature and adventure but also gain deeper understanding of Ghana’s rich cultural and archaeological history.
